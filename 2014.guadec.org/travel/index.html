Travel

There are several ways to get to Strasbourg: by plane, train and bus.

By Plane

Strasbourg International Airport (SXB)

  • 15 km from the city centre
  • From there you can take the train to the Strasbourg main train station. Up to 4 trains an hour, it’s a 9 minutes trip.

Baden Airpark (FKB)

  • 60 km from city centre
  • As explained on this page on the airport site, you can reach Strasbourg by train.

Frankfurt Airport (FRA)

  • big airport
  • trains and buses take up to around 2 hours from Frankfurt Airport to Strasbourg
  • may require a Schengen visa from a German embassy if first point of entry into the Schengen area

Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport (CDG) and Paris Orly Airport (ORY)

  • big airports
  • trains available from airports to Strasbourg

By Bus

Strasbourg is part of the European bus network: Student Agency and Eurolines provide buses from many European cities.

By Train

Strasbourg has good train connections to several European cities. You can check the exact time schedule by using the journey planner of SNCF.

Local Transportation

To get to the venue from the main train station, take the tram C and get off at the Broglie tram station.

You can find more information about local transport at CTS’s site.

Visa Information

The organization team is happy to help attendees that need visas to enter France by sending invitation letters to GUADEC. Please note these letters are not strictly required and do NOT guarantee visa approval. They are only meant to help you prove the reasons you are travelling to France. Check our wiki for details.
